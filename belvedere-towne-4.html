Answer:This problem involves counting the number of possible routes between three towns, where each route passes through a second town and does not repeat any roads. Let's break it down into steps:

Step 1: Identify the number of roads between each pair of towns. We have 3 roads from Town A to Town B and 4 roads from Town B to Town C.

Step 2: Determine the number of possible routes from Town A to Town C through Town B without passing through the same road twice. To do this, we can use the multiplication principle, which states that if we have m ways of doing one thing and n ways of doing another, then we have m x n ways of doing both things in sequence.

So, we can start by choosing one of the 3 roads from Town A to Town B, then choose one of the 4 roads from Town B to Town C, and finally choose one of the 2 remaining roads from Town C back to Town A. This gives us a total of:

3 x 4 x 2 = 24

possible routes from Town A to Town C and back to Town A, through Town B, without passing through the same road twice.

Therefore, there are 24 possible routes to take.

13. A motorist took 1/4 h to drive from town A to Town B at a speed of 90 km/h. He then took another 2/5 h to drive from Town B to Town C at a speed of 86 hm/h. (a) Find the distance he travelled between Town A and Town B. (b) Find the distance he travelled between Town B and Town C. (c) Find the total distance he travelled. ​


Answer:

(a) To find the distance travelled between Town A and Town B, we can use the formula:

Distance = Speed × Time

Here, the speed is 90 km/h, and the time taken is 1/4 h. Substituting these values in the formula, we get:

Distance = 90 × 1/4

Distance = 22.5 km

Therefore, the distance travelled between Town A and Town B is 22.5 km.

(b) To find the distance travelled between Town B and Town C, we can use the same formula:

Distance = Speed × Time

Here, the speed is 86 km/h, and the time taken is 2/5 h. Substituting these values in the formula, we get:

Distance = 86 × 2/5

Distance = 34.4 km

Therefore, the distance travelled between Town B and Town C is 34.4 km.

(c) To find the total distance travelled, we can simply add the distances travelled between Town A and Town B and between Town B and Town C:

Total Distance = Distance AB + Distance BC

Total Distance = 22.5 + 34.4

Total Distance = 56.9 km

Therefore, the total distance travelled is 56.9 km.

17. a tourist bus left town A at 9:00 AM and arrived in Town B at 4:00 PM. What is the average speed of the tourist bus if the distance between the two towns is 672 km?​


Answer:

96km/h.

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the average speed of the tourist bus, we need to divide the total distance traveled by the time taken:

Average speed = Total distance / Time taken

The time taken by the tourist bus is calculated by subtracting the departure time from the arrival time:

Time taken = Arrival time - Departure time

The arrival time is 4:00 PM, which is 16:00 in 24-hour time format. The departure time is 9:00 AM, which is 9:00 in 24-hour time format.

Time taken = 16:00 - 9:00 = 7 hours

Now we can calculate the average speed:

Average speed = 672 km / 7 hours

Average speed = 96 km/h

Therefore, the average speed of the tourist bus is 96 km/h.

22. if there are 3 roads from Town A to Town B and 4 roads from Town B to Town C. How many ways can one go from Town A to C and back to Town A through the Town B without passing through the same road twice?


This problem can be solved using the Fundamental Principle of Counting or FPC. The FPC states that if one event can be done in [tex]m[/tex] ways and another event can be done in [tex]n[/tex] ways, the number of ways that the two events can happen is [tex]mn[/tex] ways. For example, if you have 2 different shirts and 5 different pair of pants, the number of possible combinations of a shirt and a pair pants that you can make is  2×5 = 10 ways.

Now, to solve this problem, we have to remember that the route is this: From A to B, then from B to C, then from C back to B, and from B back to A. 

Let's count our choices:
(a) From A to B, we have 3 roads to choose from;
(b) From B to C, we have 4 roads to choose from;
(c) In going back from C to B, we only have 3 options this time since we already took one in (b), again we cannot take the same road twice;
(d) From B to A, we only have 2 roads to choose from since we already took one in (a).

Therefore, applying the FPC now, we have a total of 3×4×3×2 = 72 ways.
