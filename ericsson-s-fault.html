13. similarities and difference of the theories of freud, ericsson , jung , and adler in terms of their concept about personal development​


Answer:

Freud, Jung and Adler are influential theorists that have specific positions across human nature, problem formation, change and techniques as it relates to personality theories. Freud's theory is referred to as psycho-analysis, Jung's is analytical psychology and Adler's is individual psychology.

Explanation:

Adler-Adler also believed in the importance of social connections, seeing childhood development,emerging through social development rather than the sexual stages.

Freud-Freud believed that we are motivated by sexual and aggressive urges.

Ericsson- Emphasized the social relationships that are important at each stage of personality development

Jung-(1964) believed that through biology, each person is handed down the same themes and that the same types of symbols,such as the hero,The task of integrating these unconscious archetypal aspects of the self is part of the self-realization process in the second half of life.
