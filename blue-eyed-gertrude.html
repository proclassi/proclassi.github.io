Answer:

Heterozygous - A diploid organism is heterozygous at a gene locus when its cells contain two different alleles (one wild-type allele and one mutant allele) of a gene. 3. A women that is heterozygous for brown eyes marries a blue eyed man. What is the probability of them having a brown eyed child and of a blue eyed child?


Alleles:
B - blue eyes
b - brown eyes

Shown in the punnett square above, if a woman that is heterozygous dominant with brown eyes marries a blue-eyed man, the offsprings will be:

BB: BB: Bb: Bb

2 BB: 2 Bb

50%: 50%

The probability of them having a brown-eyed child or a blue-eyed child is 2 out of 4 or 50/50.

4. 1. In humans, brown eyes (B) are dominant over blue (b)*. A brown-eyed man marries a blue-eyed woman and they have three children, two of whom are brown-eyed and one of whom is blue-eyed. a. Draw the Punnett square that illustrates this marriage. What is the man’s genotype? What are the genotypes and phenotypes of the children? b. If blue is recessive, what must the woman’s genotype be? c. If that’s her genotype, what kind(s) of gametes (eggs) can she produce?d. If the man has brown eyes, but has a blue-eyed child what must his genotype be? e. If that’s his genotype, what kind(s) of gametes (sperm) can he produce?


a. The man's genotype is Bb.
Phenotypes of the offsprings:
- heterozygous brown-eyed
- homozygous blue eyed
Genotypes of the offsprings:
- Bb
- bb

b. The blue is recessive, so the woman's genotype is bb. It is impossible for her to have B because it is dominant over b.

c. The woman can only produce bb or blue-eyed.

d. The man's genotype is Bb. It is impossible for him to produce an offspring with blue-eyes if his genotype is BB.

e. The man can produce Bb or brown-eyed and bb or blue-eyed.
