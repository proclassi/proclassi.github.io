Answer:

Explanation:

let x = first night

x + 440 = second night

x + 880 = third night

x + (x + 440) + (x +880) = 6600

3x + 1320 = 6600

3x = 6600 - 1320

3x = 5280

x = 5280 / 3

x = 1760

substituting terms,

1760 (first night)

1760 + 440 = 2200 (2nd night)

1760 + 880 = 2640 (3rd night)

to check:

1760 + 2200 + 2640 = 6600

12. A metal cube, 2.00 cm on each side, has a density of 6600 kg/m^3. Find its apparent mass when it is totally submerged in water.


Answer:

The mass of the metal cube is 0.0528 kg or 52.8 grams.

Explanation:

Formula for density

ρ [tex]=\frac{m}{V}[/tex]

where

ρ      is the density, unit is in kg/[tex]m^3[/tex]

[tex]m[/tex]     is the mass, unit is in kg

[tex]V[/tex]     is the volume, unit is in [tex]m^3[/tex]

For the given information

s = 2.00 cm

ρ = 6600 kg/[tex]m^3[/tex]

m = ?

Solving the problem

1. Convert 2.00 cm ⇒ meter

  2.00 cm x [tex]\frac{1m}{100cm}[/tex] = 0.02 m

  s = 0.02 m

2. Solve for the volume of cube

  [tex]V_{cube} =s^3[/tex]

  [tex]V_{cube} = (0.02m)^3[/tex]

  [tex]V_{cube} =8x10^{-6} m^3[/tex]

3. Use the density formula to solve for the mass of the metal cube

   ρ [tex]=\frac{m}{V}[/tex]

   6600 kg/[tex]m^3[/tex] = [tex]\frac{m}{8x10^{-6} m^3}[/tex]

   m = (6600 kg/[tex]m^3[/tex])([tex]8x10^{-6} m^3[/tex])

   m = 0.0528 kg or 52.8 grams

Therefore, the mass of the metal cube is 0.0528 kg or 52.8 grams.
