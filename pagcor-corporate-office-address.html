23. 1. Briefly describe what is considered a conglomerate or group of companies. 2. Briefly discuss the Corporate Level Strategies. 3. Discuss the concept of diversified companies and conglomerates conceming corporate strategy. 4. Identify and briefly explain the 4 E's to addressing Corporate Strategy. 5.What are the strategic choices at the corporate level?​


1. A conglomerate or group of companies is a large corporation that consists of multiple subsidiary companies that operate in different industries or sectors. These subsidiaries may operate independently or may be integrated into the parent company's operations.

2. Corporate Level Strategies are the strategies that a company uses to manage its portfolio of businesses and determine the appropriate level of resource allocation to each business. These strategies include growth, stability, and retrenchment strategies. Growth strategies include diversification, acquisitions, and mergers. Stability strategies involve maintaining the current level of operations and improving efficiency. Retrenchment strategies involve downsizing or divesting businesses that are underperforming.

3. Diversified companies and conglomerates often use corporate strategy to manage their portfolio of businesses. Diversified companies operate in multiple industries, while conglomerates may operate in unrelated industries. These companies use corporate strategy to determine which businesses to invest in and how to allocate resources among them. They may also use corporate strategy to identify potential synergies between their businesses and to manage risk across their portfolio.

4. The 4 E's to addressing Corporate Strategy are:

- Environment: understanding the external environment in which the company operates and identifying opportunities and threats

- Enterprise: assessing the company's strengths and weaknesses, including its resources and capabilities

- Execution: developing and implementing strategies to achieve the company's goals

- Evaluation: monitoring and evaluating the effectiveness of the company's strategies and making adjustments as needed

5. The strategic choices at the corporate level include:

- Growth strategy: This strategy involves expanding the company's operations through mergers, acquisitions, or diversification. The objective of this strategy is to increase the company's revenue, market share, and profitability by entering new markets or expanding its product or service offerings.

- Stability strategy: This strategy involves maintaining the current level of operations and improving efficiency. The objective of this strategy is to stabilize the company's financial performance and maintain its competitive position in the market.

- Retrenchment strategy: This strategy involves downsizing or divesting businesses that are underperforming. The objective of this strategy is to reduce the company's costs, improve its financial performance, and focus on its core competencies.

- Combination strategy: This strategy involves using a mix of growth, stability, and retrenchment strategies in different parts of the company's portfolio. The objective of this strategy is to balance the company's risk and reward by investing in growth opportunities while maintaining stability in its core operations.
