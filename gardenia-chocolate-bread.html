22. Situation 2: Gardenia or rosal plant placed inside the dark room. Direction: Write an observation based on the given situation. 1. 2. ACTIVITY 3: Formulating a Hypothesis Situation 1: Gardenia plant placed under the sun. Situation 2: Gardenia plant placed inside the dark room. 1. 2.​


GARDENIA OR ROSAL PLANT IN THE DARK ROOM

Hypothesis: The plants need light for it to grow

Results:

Flowers die without oxygen and light in a week.

Observations

They will stop developing and become pale or yellowish if they do not receive enough light. They could perhaps not bloom at all. That occurs because plants require light for photosynthesis; otherwise, they begin to wilt and finally perish for lack of food and nutrients.

Reasons for the wilting of the flower

The nourishment that plants require to grow, reproduce, and live would not be available to them without the light. For plants to survive, three things are essential: carbon dioxide, water, and sunshine. The plants utilize the energy from the sun to transform carbon dioxide, soil nutrients, and water into food in a process known as photosynthesis.

Context

Although a Gardenia plant enjoys full sun, some shade is preferred during the hotter months of the year since too much sunshine can cause its leaves to burn and its buds to drop off. Gardenias do well in hot regions with early light and late shade.Gardenias typically thrive in full sun, but in the warmest regions of their hardiness zone, they may benefit from shade during the hottest time of the day. They don't appear to thrive in dry or arid environments and tend to thrive in humid environments.

ANSWER:

Unfortunately, bread has a short shelf life because it is vulnerable to mold growth. And even when it is still safe to eat, bread goes stale quite quickly. The reason why bread goes stale is that the starches within the product start degrading. Because of that, packing the bread before storage is so important.

MORE:

However, bread will generally become stale past its expiration date, but it's still safe to eat. If it's moldy, toss it out.

According to “sell by” dates, bread is good for three to five days after opening, but it can actually be consumed much longer after that as long as there is no mold growth. You can usually see fuzzy, green spots on the surface of bread, so it's easy to tell when it's time to toss.

Loaves you get from a bakery and homemade bread have a shorter shelf life because they're free of the preservatives found in commercially produced sliced bread and rolls. Breads with added fat, however, like brioche or challah, will stand up to staleness a bit longer.

When it comes to storing bread, the refrigerator is your worst enemy. Bread actually goes stale faster in the refrigerator than it does at room temperature. The best way to keep bread at its best is to keep it at room temperature for a day or two, then wrap it up and freeze it for longer-term storage.
