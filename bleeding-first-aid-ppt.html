1. enumeration first aid for nose bleeds​


Answer:

Pinch your nose.

Use your thumb and index finger to pinch your nostrils shut. Breathe through your mouth. Continue to pinch for 10 to 15 minutes. Pinching sends pressure to the bleeding point on the nasal septum and often stops the flow of blood.

Explanation:

1.)Severe bleeding: First aid

Ways of giving first aid for open wounds with severe bleeding:

1. Carefully remove any debris or things stuck on the wound.

2. If possible, clean the severe wound carefully with clean water.

3. Try to stop the bleeding, such as putting a clean cloth or bandage over it and pressing it firmly.

4.If the severe bleeding stops, don't remove the first bandage covering it. Instead add another layer of clean bandage or clean cloth to cover it.

5. Use a tourniquet, if you have knowledge in using it, to stop the bleeding. (A tourniquet is a device that is used to apply pressure to a limb or extremity in order to stop the flow of blood. )

6. Call a health emergency service to get proper and professional help.

7. Do not apply a tourniquet to stop the bleeding of a wound. is it inappropriate fisrt aid treatment?​


Answer:

Tourniquets are tight bands used to completely stop the blood flow to a wound. To control bleeding after an injury to a limb, tourniquets should ideally only be used by first responders trained in emergency first aid. Knowing when (and when not) to use a tourniquet to control bleeding can be difficult to ascertain.

Anyone can apply a tourniquet. While you do not need any official or special medical certification or training, you do need to understand how to properly use one.

Explanation:

Before you apply a tourniquet, you need to determine the source of the bleed. In some cases, such as near or complete limb amputation, it may be obvious. Other injuries may not be visible at first, especially if there is debris, wreckage, tattered clothing, or other objects obstructing your view.

If possible, have the injured person lay down so you can assess them from head to toe. Try to stay calm and focused, as you will need to find the source of the bleeding as quickly as you can.

Position the cloth, towel, or other material to be used for the tourniquet on the limb several inches above the injury. You will want to position the tourniquet at the part of the limb that is closest to the heart. For example, if the injury is below the knee or elbow, you will need to tie the tourniquet above the joint.

Use a common square knot (like tying your shoelaces, but without making a bow) to tie the tourniquet around the limb.

13. you are slicing an eggplant and suddenly, the knife slips and your index finger get minor cut what the first aid treatment will you do to stop it bleeding?​


Answer:

1 Wash your hands with soap and water.

2 Clean the wound with warm water and soap or another mild cleanser to get any dirt away from the cut.

3 Carefully use tweezers cleaned with rubbing alcohol to remove pieces of glass, gravel, or other debris from the wound.

4 Apply firm, but gentle pressure to the wound with a clean cloth or gauze pad.

5 Add another layer if blood soaks through the cloth or pad.

6 Raise the finger above the heart, allowing the hand or arm to rest on something if needed.

7 Once the bleeding has stopped, which should take a few minutes at most for a minor cut, take the covering away to let it start healing.

8 Apply a little petroleum jelly (Vaseline) to help reduce scarring and speed up healing.

9 Leave the cut uncovered if it’s not likely to get dirty or rub against clothes or other surfaces.

10 Cover the cut with an adhesive strip, such as a Band-Aid, if the cut is on a part of your finger that may get dirty or touch other surfaces.
