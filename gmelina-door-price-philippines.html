CONE-BEARING PLANTS

Answer:

A. Pine tree

Explanation:

Some examples of cone-bearing plants are conifers and cycads. Cycads are woody palmlike trees which can grow up to 20 meters. They grow large cones when they mature. Conifers are woody trees with needle-like leaves that reduce water loss during dry or cold climates. They develope many cones and one tree can produce both male and female cones.

Cone-bearing plants have their seeds exposed in cones. The cones are composed of woody scales that form diagonal rows with each row overlapping the next. The male cones produce the pollen grains with sperm cells, while female cones contain the ovules with two to three egg cells.

The pollen grains are very small and are encased in waterproof wrapping that can be carried away by insects or the wind. The seeds that contain the female sex cells, on the other hand, are protected within the cone and do not leave the plant.
