7. list down ten habits for good delivery that a speaker needs to develop


List down ten habits for good delivery that a speaker needs to develop

1. NERVOUSNESS IS NATURAL. PRACTICE AND PREPARATION!

All feels certain physiological responses, such as beating hearts and shaky hands. Don't equate these emotions with the sense that you're going to do badly or making a fool of yourself. Some of the nerves are healthy. The adrenaline rush that makes you sweat makes you more conscious and able to do well. The easiest way to overcome fear is to plan, prepare, and prepare for more. Take the time to read through the notes a couple times. If you've made yourself familiar with the stuff, practice—a ton. Video tape yourself, or get a friend to criticize your performance.

2. KNOW YOUR AUDIENCE. YOUR SPEECH IS ABOUT THEM, NOT YOU.

Before you start composing your message, remember who the message is about. Know as much as you can from your listeners. This will help you decide your use of words, your level of detail, your organisation pattern, and your motivational message.

3. ORGANIZE THE MATERIAL AT THE MOST EFFICIENT MANNER TO ATTAIN YOUR REASON.

Establish the context of your speech. Write down the subject, the general intent, the basic purpose, the core concept and the key points. Be sure you get the attention of the audience in the first 30 seconds..

4. WATCH FOR FEEDBACK AND ADAPT TO IT.

Keep your attention on the listener. Gauge your responses, change your greeting, and be flexible. Delivering a canned speech will mean that you lose your concentration or annoy even the most dedicated listeners.

5. LET YOUR PERSONALITY COME THROUGH.

Be yourself, don't be a talking head—in any kind of conversation. You would be more trustworthy if your personality comes through, and your viewers will believe what you have to say if they can see you as a real person.

6. USE HUMOR, TELL STORIES, AND USE EFFECTIVE LANGUAGE.

Inject a funny joke in your pitch, and you're guaranteed to attract the attention of your audience. Generally, people want a personal touch in a speech. That's what a story should do.

7. DON’T READ UNLESS YOU HAVE TO. WORK FROM AN OUTLINE.

Reading a script or a slide fractures the emotional connection. By keeping eye contact with the listener, you keep your attention on yourself and your message. A simple description should be used to jog your memory and keep you focused.

8. USE YOUR VOICE AND HANDS EFFECTIVELY. OMIT NERVOUS GESTURES.

Non-verbal communication brings much of the message. Good delivery does not draw attention to itself, but instead conveys the thoughts of the speaker simply and without distraction.

9. GRAB ATTENTION AT THE BEGINNING, AND CLOSE WITH A DYNAMIC END.

10. USE AUDIOVISUAL AIDS WISELY.

Too many participants can break a strong link to the audience, so use them sparingly. They should improve or explain your content, or attract and retain the attention of your audience.

15. Write down five things you would prohibit audience from doing during a presentation or a delivery of a speech.


walking out

Don't take this personally because chances are it has nothing to do with you. He or she may have to use the bathroom, or just received a text they need to react to. They may even leave because what you're talking about isn't something they want or need to hear. This still has nothing to do with you. So ignore them, let them go, and keep talking to those who have remained to hear your words of wisdom.

getting booed

if you do get booed, it’s best to figure out why it’s happening (or afterwards, when you reflect on your speech, and analyze what worked and didn’t work about it, why it happened). That might help you to see if there’s something you said that touched off that reaction that you might reconsider putting in your speech again or, if it’s pivotal to your speech.

interrupting you

this behavior is unacceptable, ask him/her why, they may have a good reason.

audience won't listen

don't force them. let them listen if they want to! what's important is you deliver your speech to those who want to listen.

making too much noise

remind them that they are in an area wherein people are interested in hearing a speech or presentation. don't lose your cool and handle them calmly. they may be seeking attention to let people know his opinion. or maybe they don't agree with your presentation, if this is unintentional ask them to tone it down and/or ask for them to do what their doing someplace else.

30. Write down five things you would prohibit performers from doing during a presentation or delivery of speech.


Delivering a speech is not an easy thing to do. You are going to speak in front of people who have diverse backgrounds. Aside from this, if one has stage fright, then he/she has to overcome it to be able to effectively and efficiently deliver the speech.

Stage fright is just an umbrella term for the problems a speaker faces during speaking. It is a general term which covers a lot of specific problems. And these problems should be avoided or prohibited during speech delivery. Below are specific problems that need not to be encountered by speakers. It should be avoided and prohibited.

No eye contact - A speaker should avoid this one to be able to create connection with the audience.Use of fillers - "Uhhhm" "ahhh" are most common fillers used by speakers who have stage fright. This should be avoided and prohibited so that people won't think you are unprepared or you are not familiar of your speech. If this happens people might lose interest listening to you.Talking or speaking very fast - It is very important that the audience digest every bit of what you say. If the speaker speaks like it is as if a race, people will get confused and understand nothing.Standing stiff - When nervousness takes over the speaker, he or she has the tendency to stand stiff or acts like a robot. In front of the people you have to loosen up and do gestures to avoid appearing like a robot.Stuttering - This also should be avoided and prohibited to make a good impression to the audience.

All these things are products of stage fright. One simple thing to overcome this is to be confident and practice enough.
