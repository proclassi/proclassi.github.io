15. how to properly use a drawing paper, Masking tape, Erasing shield, t-squared, and a drawing table/board?


Using drawing paper, masking tape, erasing shield, T-square, and a drawing table/board can help you create precise and accurate drawings. Here are some tips on how to use these tools properly:

Drawing paper: Choose the appropriate size and type of drawing paper for your project. Make sure the paper is clean and free from any creases or folds that could interfere with your drawing.

Masking tape: Use masking tape to secure your paper to the drawing board or table. This will prevent the paper from moving around while you're drawing, and will also create a neat, clean edge to your drawing.

Erasing shield: An erasing shield is a small metal or plastic stencil that you can place over your drawing to protect certain areas from erasure. This is especially useful when you're working on a detailed drawing and want to keep certain lines or areas of the drawing clean and unmarked.

T-square: A T-square is a long, straightedge tool that you can use to draw straight lines. To use a T-square, align the top of the T-square with the edge of your paper, then hold the bottom of the T-square and draw your line along the straightedge.

Drawing table/board: A drawing table or board provides a flat, stable surface for your drawing. Make sure the table or board is at a comfortable height for you to work on, and that it's positioned in a well-lit area to help you see your work clearly.

When using these tools together, start by taping your paper to the drawing board using masking tape. Use a T-square to draw straight lines, and an erasing shield to protect areas of your drawing that you want to keep clean. Remember to work slowly and carefully, taking the time to ensure that your lines are straight and your drawing is precise. With practice, you'll be able to create detailed, accurate drawings that you can be proud of.

18. Why is masking tape the most preferred adhesive when fastening the drawing paper to the drawing?​


Answer:

Because it is intended to hold blueprints to a drawing board or light table, and to pull off easily without damaging the drawing. House painter's tape is rated by how many days it can be left up without leaving residue on the surface being masked.

27. Why is masking tape used in drawing instead of other type of fastening materials​


Answer:

Perhaps the most significant difference between masking and painter's tapes is that painter's tapes remove cleanly after the job is done – up to a certain amount of time – while masking tapes tend to leave residue behind if left up for the duration of a paint project.
