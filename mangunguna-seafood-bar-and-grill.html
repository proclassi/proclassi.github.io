6. Activity 3: Compare Me Directions: Using a Venn Diagram, compare and contrast the different cooking methods of seafood. a. Baking and braising b. Broiling and grilling c. Sautéing and panfrying d. Poaching and steaming​


Answer:

1. Baking and Braising

a. Comparison

Both are used for slow-cooking vegetables and meat without using much oil or having them soggy if submerged in water.

b. Contrast

Baking uses less water and more oil, meanwhile, braising requires liquid.

2. Broiling and grilling

a. Comparison

Grilling and broiling both use intense direct heat to cook foods.

b. Contrast

When grilling, the heat source is below (like with a barbecue grill), but in oven broiling, the heating source is above.

3. Sautéing and panfrying

a. Comparison

Both method uses oil.

b. Contrast

Sautéing needs minimal oil only and fries herbs or spices such as onion and garlic. Panfrying needs higher amount of oil than sautéing. It involves only the oil.  

4. Poaching and steaming

a. Comparison

It uses water and only collects heat from the heated water and not directly in the water. This helps not break delicate protein.

b. Contrast

Poaching is when eggs, vegetables, and meat are submerged into water. Similar to boiling.

29. A small amount of hot fat is used in cooking fish/seafood in this method. Select one: a. Poaching b. Pan frying c. Deep frying d. Simmering e. Broiling/Grilling f. Baking g. Baking/Roasting h. Steaming i. Boiling j. Sauteeing


Answer:

A small amount of hot fat is used in cooking fish/seafood in this method.

e. Broiling/Grilling

Explanation:

Cooking Methods for Fish or Seafood

There are different methods used for cooking fish or any seafoods. The method used depends on the thickness of the fish, natural flavor or the level of fat in the meat.

Some of the cooking methods of fish that we can use are the following:

Baking - It is one of the safest ways to cook fish. Fish with thicker and oilier fish is ideal for baking because the heat of a hot oven can dry out more delicate fish.

Broiling or Grilling - This is the method where a small amount of hot fat is used in cooking fish.

Poaching - This is excellent for light fish, as it prevents the fish from drying out, and it helps to enhance the delicate flavor.

Steaming - This healthy method stops fish drying out as the heat is moist and indirect. This method is also for many types of shellfish.

Pan frying - It is a good choice for delicate and flaky fish. This cooking method creates a crispy crust.

Deep frying - It is the method for fish with a neutral flavor. Typically, any type of fish with white meat will fry well.
