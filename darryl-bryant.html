3. What is the summary of The Preludes by Darryl Delgado ?


It's a winter evening in a city. The speaker addresses us, narrating the wet newspapers that hit our feet and the broken, dirty features of the city around us. As night falls, people begin to light their lamps. In the second stanza, it's morning, and we consider the people rising early to rush out into the mud and get to work. Night falls again in stanza 3, and the speaker narrates our insomnia. Sordid thoughts preoccupy our mind until dawn, when we notice our hands and feet are dirty from the day before. In stanza 4, we arrive in the business center of town at the end of a workday. A soul floats around unnoticed, occasionally winding up trampled under feet of people leaving work for the day. The speaker feels emotionally moved by this, and we flash back to an image of the past, where women are gathering fuel to start a fire.

If you are referring to the philosopher and cultural critic, Kobe Bryant, he did not develop a formal philosophical system or principles. However, he did share some personal views on life and success that may be considered as philosophical insights. Here are three possible principles that can be inferred from his statements and actions:

1.    Mamba Mentality: Kobe Bryant coined the term "Mamba Mentality" to describe his approach to basketball and life. It involves intense focus, relentless pursuit of excellence, and willingness to overcome obstacles and failures. He believed that success is not just about talent but also about hard work, discipline, and mental toughness.

2.    Growth Mindset: Kobe Bryant also emphasized the importance of continuous learning and improvement. He was known to read extensively, seek feedback, and experiment with new skills and techniques. He believed that one should never be satisfied with one's current level of performance or knowledge but should always strive to expand one's potential.

3.    Legacy and Purpose: Kobe Bryant also reflected on the deeper meaning and legacy of one's actions. He saw basketball as a way to inspire and connect with people across cultures and generations. He also pursued other creative and entrepreneurial ventures, such as writing, filmmaking, and investing, to express his vision and values. He believed that one's purpose in life is not just to achieve personal success but also to contribute to the greater good and leave a positive impact on the world.
