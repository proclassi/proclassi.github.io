23. In connection to the case titled "Friendship in Business," was the action of Mr. Dy to cancel the contact legally correct and morally justified?


Answer:

No.

Explanation:

As what I have observed while reading the case, right after the molasses was returned, the rage or anger that Mr. Dy felt was the factor that leads to the cancellation of contract which is considered as not legally correct and not morally justified action. He should not let his emotions ruin their relationship as business associates. In fact, Mr. Gutierrez has the right to return the molasses since it did not meet the indicated quality. Of course, as an owner of Philippine-Hawaiian International Sugarcane Incorporated, it is your job to ensure that the products that you are going to deliver to your clients are in a high quality. Just because Mr. Gutierrez’s payment is delayed, it doesn’t mean that you will send him a substandard or low quality product. In the very first place, it was Mr. Dy who agreed with his friend that it’s okay that the payment will be delayed. So, it’s not clearly reasonable to argue that the delay of payment was a form of breach of contract. He cannot blame anyone for his actions. In this case, the right thing that Mr. Dy should have done is to replace those molasses with better quality, and then deliver it right away to Mr. Gutierrez. Only if he did it, there will be no problem at all. The said contract and their relationship as business associates will still remain.
