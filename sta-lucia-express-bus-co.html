6. A map showing the locations of different municipalities and cities is drawn on a coordinate plane is equivalent to 6 kilometers. Suppose the coordinates of Mabini City is (2, 2) and Sta. Lucia town is (6, 8). What is the shortest distance between these two places?​


[tex]\Large\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Statement: A map showing the locations of different municipalities and cities is drawn on a coordinate plane is equivalent to 6 kilometers. Suppose the coordinates of Mabini City is (2, 2) and Sta. Lucia town is (6, 8).

Required: What is the shortest distance between these two places?

Let,

[tex]\sf{(x_1, \: y_1) = (2, \: 2)}[/tex][tex]\sf{(x_2, \: y_2) = (6, \: 8)}[/tex]

[tex]\bold{Formula : } \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \\ \boxed{ \rm \: Distance = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2 \: } }[/tex]

Substitute the given values of x and y into the above formula and solve for Distance:

[tex]\sf: \implies{Distance = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf: \implies{Distance = \sqrt{(6 - 2)^2 + (8 - 2)^2}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf: \implies{Distance = \sqrt{(4)^2 + (6)^2}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf: \implies{Distance = \sqrt{16 + 36}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf: \implies{Distance = \sqrt{52}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf: \implies{Distance = \sqrt{4(13)}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf: \implies{Distance = \bold{2\sqrt{13} \approx 7.21 \: units}}[/tex]

Since, each unit on the coordinate plane is equivalent to 6 kilometers, we multiply the obtained value of distance by 6 to get the shortest distance between Mabini City and Sta. Lucia town.

[tex]\sf:\implies{Distance = 7.21 \: units \times 6 \: km = \bold{43.26 \: km}}[/tex]

Therefore, the shortest distance between Mabini City and Sta. Lucia town is approximatey 43.26 kilometers.
