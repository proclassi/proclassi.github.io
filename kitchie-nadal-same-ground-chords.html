[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

One theorem suggests that: "The perpendicular bisector of the chord passes through the center". Now we already knew that a diameter passes through the center, so we can say that:

[tex] \rm If \: diameter \perp chord, [/tex]

[tex] \rm then \: the \: diameter \: bisects \: the \: chord. [/tex]

Simply put, if and only if the diameter is perpendicular to the chord, then the diameter is the perpendicular bisector of the chord.

These are called slash chords. For instance, C/F indicates that an C major triad should be played with an added F in the bass. In some genres of modern jazz, two chords with a slash between them may indicate an advanced chord type called a polychord, which is the playing of two chords simultaneously.
