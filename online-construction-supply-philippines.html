30. Construct perfectly elastic and inelastic supply curves.


A perfectly elastic supply curve is a theoretical supply curve that represents a situation in which the quantity of a good or service that a supplier is willing to produce is infinitely responsive to changes in the price of the good or service. In other words, if the price of a good or service increases, the supplier will be willing to produce an infinite quantity of the good or service, and if the price decreases, the supplier will be willing to produce zero units.

A perfectly inelastic supply curve is a theoretical supply curve that represents a situation in which the quantity of a good or service that a supplier is willing to produce is completely unresponsive to changes in the price of the good or service. In other words, no matter how the price of the good or service changes, the supplier will always be willing to produce the same quantity of the good or service.
