To know what time in Egypt will the plane arrive, first, we will subtract 6 hours from 6:00 a.m. since the time in the Philippines is 6 hours ahead of Egypt. Then, add 11 hours to the difference.

06:00 – 6 hours = 00:00 --24 hour clock format

13. Define the cultures and traditions of Southeast Asian countries that follow where the rules of parallelism are applied.• Philippines, Thailand, Egypt​


Answer:

Parallelism is the grammatical rule that requires the use of the same grammatical form in a sentence, usually used in a series or in comparisons.

In terms of the cultures and traditions of the Southeast Asian countries mentioned, the following information applies:

Philippines:

In traditional Filipino culture, parallelism is often used in proverbs or "salawikain." These are short sayings that offer advice or convey moral lessons. Many salawikain use parallelism to make them more memorable and effective in conveying their message.

Example: "Ang buhay ay parang gulong, minsan nasa ibabaw, minsan nasa ilalim." (Life is like a wheel, sometimes on top, sometimes at the bottom.)

Thailand:

In Thai literature and poetry, parallelism is a commonly used literary device. It is used to convey harmony, balance, and symmetry. Thai parallelism often features rhyming phrases in order to create a musical effect.

Example: "ฝนตกหนัก สุดเสียงฉาง เช้าใบยังเปียก ริมน้ำมืดมน" (Heavy rain pours with thunder, the morning leaves still wet, and the banks of the river are dark.)

Egypt:

Egyptian parallelism is often used in religious texts and poetry. It is used to emphasize the importance of ideas and to make them more memorable. In ancient Egypt, parallelism was used in hymns and prayers to the gods, while in modern times, it is used in popular songs and poetry.

Example: "انت الحبيب و انت المحبوب و انت الذي تلين القلوب و تغسل الذنوب" (You are the beloved, you are the lover, and you are the one who softens hearts and washes sins.)
