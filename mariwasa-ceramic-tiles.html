5. A wall facing, usually of wood, cut stone, or ceramic tile, that is carried only partway up a wall.


Wainscoting is about a wall facing that is only carried partially up a wall, typically made of wood, cut stone, or ceramic tile.

Main Types of Wainscoting:

Board and Batten Wainscoting - Wainscoting with vertical boards used to conceal the seams or styles is known as board and batten wainscoting. Distinctive in style thanks to its consistent pattern of broad vertical molding boards, or battens. These battens are typically positioned 6 to 10 inches apart and range in width from 4 to 6 inches. Additionally, battens were used to hide the joints between the boards; extra battens might be inserted between the joints for aesthetic purposes.

Raised Panel Wainscoting - In order to insulate homes, raised panel wainscoting was initially made popular in England in the 17th century. The formal dining and living rooms are the most typical locations for this sort of wainscoting, which is also the oldest and most prevalent.

Flat Panel Wainscoting - Panels in flat panel wainscoting are positioned behind stiles and rails. This form of wainscoting has a more modern appearance because it employs boards without beveled edges or molding, which makes them appear deeper than the stiles and rails.

Overlay Wainscoting - Combining flat panel and raised panel wainscoting styles results in overlay wainscoting. Prior to centering additional panels on top of flat panels, they are first installed. For added elegance, the lining of these extra panels can be made of molding.

Beadboard Wainscoting - The raised beads and long, continuous vertical grooves that define beadboard wainscoting are spaced around every inch to two inches apart. Thin, individual boards are arranged next to one another to create beadboard wainscoting. The tongue and groove system are used to connect these little boards, which are typically between 32 and 48 inches long and 4 inches wide.

17. what is the difference between a ceramic tiles and rubber​


Answer:

Ceramic tiles are created from clay that has been permanently hardened by heat, whereas rubber is a hard-wearing flooring material made primarily of natural or synthetic rubber with a clay filler and fibrous talc or asbestos as a filler.
