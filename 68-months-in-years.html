Answer:

Leonard: 21 1/7 ; Sheldon: 16 4/7

Step-by-step explanation:

In this problem, we are given the relationship of two numbers, specifically ages, currently and the information when they are compared a period of time from now.

In these types of problems, our main priority is to find the working equation that can help us give values to our variables. Saying that, let's declare our variables first.

Let x be the age of Leonard. From the first sentence of the question, we can imply that Sheldon's age is 4x - 68.

Now, we can work on the second sentence of the question, which will be very helpful in determining the equation.

If you correctly turn the sentence into symbols and numbers, you will get the following equation:

x + 12 = 2[(4x - 68) + 12] - 24

Solving for x,

x + 12 = 2 (4x - 56) - 24

x + 12 = 8x - 112 - 24

-7x = -148

7x = 148

x = 148 / 7 = 21 1/7

Now, we can use this to find Sheldon's age. Remember that the latter's age is represented as 4x - 68.

We can just substitute 21 1/7 into x to find Sheldon's age.

4x - 68 = 4x - 68

4x - 68 = 4(21 1/7) - 68

4x - 68 = 4(21) + 4(1/7) - 68

4x - 68 = 84 - 68 + 4/7

4x - 68 = 16 4/7

We can check our answers by substituting our values in the working equation.

(21 1/7) + 12 = 2[(16 4/7) + 12] - 24

33 1/7 = 2 (28 4/7) - 24

33 1/7 = 57 1/7 - 24

33 1/7 = 33 1/7

LHS = RHS

Therefore, Leonard's age is 21 1/7, while Sheldon's age is 16 4/7.

20. 2. In a study of the life expectancy of 400 people in a certain geographic region, the mean age at death was 70 years, and the standard deviation was 5.1 years. If a sample of 50 people from this region is selected, what is the probability that the mean life expectancy will be less than 68 years?​


Answer:

Given:

Population mean (μ) = 70 years

Population standard deviation (σ) = 5.1 years

Sample size (n) = 50

Sample mean (x̄) = 68 years

We need to find the probability that the sample mean will be less than 68 years.

The sampling distribution of the sample means is approximately normal if the sample size is large enough. When the population standard deviation (σ) is known and the sample size (n) is greater than 30, we can use the z-score formula to calculate the probability.

z = (x̄ - μ) / (σ / sqrt(n)) z = (68 - 70) / (5.1 / sqrt(50)) z = -2 / 0.722 z = -2.77

Using a z-score table or a calculator, we can find that the probability of getting a z-score less than -2.77 is approximately 0.003.

Therefore, the probability that the mean life expectancy will be less than 68 years is approximately 0.003 or 0.3%.
