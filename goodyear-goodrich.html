Kram (1985) further proposed that the emotional bond between the mentor and protégé deepens and intimacy increases during this phase. This phase may last from two to five years as the protégé learns from the mentor and the mentor promotes and protects the protégé. The third phase, separation, involves a structural and psychological disconnection between the mentor and the protégé when functions provided by the mentor decrease, and the protégé becomes independent. In the redefinition phase, the mentor and protégé frequently develop a relationship that is more peer-like, characterized by mutual support and informal contact. While career and psychosocial functions are less evident, sponsorship from a distance, occasional counseling and coaching and ongoing friendship continue. The meetings are determined, monitored and evaluated based on clearly articulated goals and milestones. Informal mentoring relationship is more spontaneous and springs from the mentee’s intrinsic desire to become better. The choice of the mentor is based on trust and confidence. 1. The process that cured rubber, that was discovered by Charles Goodyear.​


Charles Goodyear discovered the process of vulcanization, which revolutionized the rubber industry. Prior to Goodyear's discovery in the mid-19th century, natural rubber was sticky, smelly, and prone to melting in hot weather, which limited its usefulness.

Vulcanization involves heating natural rubber with sulfur or other chemicals, which cross-links the rubber molecules and makes it much stronger, more durable, and more resistant to heat, cold, and chemicals. This process also reduces the stickiness and smell of the rubber, making it more practical for a wide range of applications, including tires, footwear, hoses, and many other products.

Goodyear's discovery of vulcanization was not immediate, however. He spent years experimenting with different methods and materials, and even went bankrupt several times in the process. Legend has it that he accidentally discovered the process while heating a mixture of rubber and sulfur over a stove, and then dropped the mixture on a hot stove, resulting in a tough, flexible material.

Goodyear's discovery transformed the rubber industry and had a significant impact on the development of modern technology. Today, vulcanized rubber is used in a wide range of products, from automotive and aerospace components to medical devices and consumer goods.
