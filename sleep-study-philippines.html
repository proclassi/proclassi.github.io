Repeated exposure to an addictivesubstance or behavior such as playing video games conditions the brain cells in key areas likethe prefrontal cortex—the area of the brain involved in planning and executing tasks—to craveand go after the substance or behavior causing the release of dopamine. The end result is being“addicted” to the source of pleasure, and in our children’s case—addiction to video games.Some scientists describe the effect of video games as “hijacking the pleasure center,” which isthe same reward circuit in the brain involved with motivation and memory. Since an addictivebehavior like VGP stimulates the same circuit, this part becomes “hijacked and overloaded,”unfavorably affecting the child’s memory, learning and motivation to acquire other knowledgeand skills. Hence, for many children, they may prefer playing their video games than doingtheir school assignments or reviewing for their exams.
