10. what is the meaning of gigabyte?​


Answer:

a unit of information equal to one billion (109) or, strictly, 230 bytes.

What is the meaning of gigabyte?​

The gigabyte is a digital information unit that is a multiple of the byte. In the International System of Units, the prefix giga denotes 109. (SI). As a result, a gigabyte equals one billion bytes. The gigabyte is abbreviated as GB.

This term applies to hard disk, solid state drive, and tape storage capacity, as well as data transmission rates, in all disciplines of research (particularly data science), engineering, business, and many areas of computer. However, in some sectors of computer science and information technology, the term is sometimes used to signify 1073741824 (10243 or 230) bytes, notably for RAM sizes. As a outcome, the term gigabyte may be confusing.

Hard disk capacities are stated and advertised by drive manufacturers using the usual metric definition of the gigabyte, however when Microsoft Windows displays the size of a 400 GB drive, it is represented as 372 GB, based on a binary interpretation. The International System of Quantities standardizes the binary prefixes that signify a succession of integer powers of 1024 to eliminate this uncertainty. A memory module branded as "1GB" has one gibibyte (1GiB) of storage capacity when these prefixes are used. The "372 GB" claimed for the hard disk is really 372 GiB, according to ISQ standards (400 GB).
