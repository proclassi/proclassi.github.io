Answer:

Luca Pacioli is called the 'father of accounting' because he wrote the first book that described double-entry accounting processes.

Explanation:

Luca Pacioli (c. 1447 – 1517) was the first person to publish detailed material on the double-entry system of accounting. He was an Italian mathematician and Franciscan friar who also collaborated with his friend Leonardo da Vinci
