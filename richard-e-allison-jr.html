As a pivotal figure in Philippine history, Ferdinand E. Marcos Jr’s plans for nation-building have played a significant role in shaping the country’s economy and political landscape. Marcos Jr’s vision for the Philippines was primarily focused on modernization, and stabilization of the nation in the global scene. In this essay, I will discuss the importance of Ferdinand E. Marcos Jr, and how his plans for nation-building have impacted the Philippines.

Marcos Jr served as the longest-serving president of the Philippines, from 1965 to 1986. During his presidency, he implemented various policies aimed at creating an industrialized and modernized Philippines. One of these policies was the ‘New Society’ policy, a development plan aimed at reducing poverty and increasing economic growth. The policy involved the creation of several bodies to oversee the implementation of developmental projects, such as the National Economic and Development Authority (NEDA), and the National Industrialization Council (NIC). These institutions aimed to coordinate and prioritize industrialization projects and ultimately drive economic development.

One of the significant achievements of Marcos Jr’s nation-building plan was the massive infrastructure development that took place in the country. As part of his vision for modernization, Marcos Jr intensified public investment in infrastructure, constructing roads, bridges, ports, and airports. These developments helped to connect remote areas with urban centers, facilitating commerce and economic growth across the country. Similarly, Marcos Jr’s administration focused on expanding education and healthcare, creating more opportunities for Filipinos to access essential services.

Another critical aspect of Marcos Jr’s nation-building plan was his foreign policy initiatives. His administration sought to strengthen ties with western countries and foster regional cooperation to promote trade and commerce. For instance, during his tenure, Marcos Jr actively courted foreign investors to the Philippines, helping to boost the country’s foreign direct investment, and improve economic growth rates.

Although Marcos Jr’s vision for nation-building was focused on modernization, his methods have often come under scrutiny, primarily due to the perceived authoritarian style of governance during his tenure. Critics point to widespread human rights violations, corruption, and an impervious regime that often disregarded the rule of law. The effects of his rule are still felt today.

In conclusion, despite the controversies that marred Ferdinand E. Marcos Jr’s administration, his plans for nation-building have had a profound impact on the Philippines. His vision for modernization and infrastructure development helped to create an environment that fostered economic growth, and significantly improved the quality of life for many Filipinos. However, he remains a highly polarizing figure in Philippine history, and his legacy often serves as a cautionary tale for the abuse of power. Nonetheless, the impact of his nation-building plan on the Philippine economy and infrastructure cannot be understated, and it remains a significant part of the country’s political and economic history.

28. Carla and Allison are sisters. They are on their way from school to home. Carla runs half the time and walks half the time. Allison runs half the distance and walks half the distance. If Carla and Allison walk at the same speed and run at the same speed, which one arrives home first?


If two sisters, Carla and Allison traveled at the same speed while running and walking from their school going to their home and Allison spent half of the distance running and the other half walking while Carla spent half of her time walking and the other running, Carla would arrive first.

Analyzing the problem:

We are asked to see who will arrive first, and this is a matter of time.Carla runs half the time and walks half the time while Allison runs half the distance and walks half the distance. Let us assume that the distance from their house to the school and vice versa is 3 kilometers, their running speed is 5m/s, and their walking speed is 2 m/s.

Solving for Carla's time:

d= 3 km

[tex]s_{walking}[/tex] = 2 m/s

[tex]s_{running}[/tex]= 5 m/s

We know that s= [tex]\frac{d}{t}[/tex]

Therefore, [tex]t_{Carla}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{1}{2} (\frac{3 km}{5m/s})[/tex] - [tex]\frac{1}{2} (\frac{3 km}{2m/s})[/tex] = 7.5 minutes

Solving for Allison's time:

[tex]t_{Allison}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{1.5 km}{5m/s}[/tex] + [tex]\frac{1.5 km}{2m/s}[/tex] = 17.5 minutes

Thus, Carla would arrive first since it will only take her 7.5 minutes, 10 minutes earlier, than Allison.
