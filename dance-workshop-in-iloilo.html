1. The name "modern dance" describes a range of techniques used in dance, workshops and dance choreography.


Modern Dance

Modern dance motions are seen as freeform and flowing and are frequently influenced by other dance genres, including ballet, folk dance, and African dance. Modern dance style is thought to be more relaxed and natural than ballet, yet it can still call for a lot of strength and core work. Modern dancers frequently perform barefoot while wearing form-hugging garments. The following characteristics of modern dance are notable:

1. Unstructured and improvised

A common description of modern dance is that it is fluid and freeform, switching between slow, languid moves and quick ones that reveal the body's contraction and expansion. Some dancers come up with their own original moves for routines or even improvise while performing.

2. It’s emotional

The emotions that the music inspires in the dances are expressed through modern dance. In essence, dancers express their emotions through a raw or passionate performance set to music.

3. It disregards tradition

Modern dancers use their body weight to propel themselves over the floor, frequently falling, tumbling, or rolling to convey rhythm rather than aiming for the weightlessness and grace of ballet.

Answer:

Dinagyang Festival

Explanation:

The Dinagyang Festival is a famous and religious and cultural festival in Iloilo City, Philippines held on the 4th Sunday of January, or right after the Sinulog in Cebu and the Ati-Atihan Festival in Kalibo, Aklan.

Answer:

Dinagyang Festival

Explanation:

Dinagyang is a Hiligaynon word that means “merrymaking.” Just like the Sinulog and Ati-Atihan Festivals, Dinagyang Festival is one of the religious festivities in the Philippines that celebrates the feast of the Santo Niño and the pact between the Datus and locals.
