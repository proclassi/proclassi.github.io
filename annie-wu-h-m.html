16. The sum of Annie's and Anna's age is 30. Anna is 8 years more than Annie. Find their age.​


Answer:

Annie is 14 and Anna is 16.

Step-by-step explanation:

The first sentence of the problem states that the sum of their ages is 30. We can treat this as a number problem by saying the sum of two numbers is 30.

If we let x to be Annie's age, then Anna's age is 30 - x.

Then, the next sentence states that Anna's age is 9 years more than half of Annie's age. By translating into a number problem, this is the same as saying 30 - x is equal to 9 plus half of x.

Then, we solve for x.

We then arrive at x = 14. Since Annie's age is x, this means that Annie is 14 years old.

Next, since Anna's age is represented by 30 - x, we substitute 14 for x and we find out that Anna is 16 years old.

We can check this: the sum of their ages, 14 + 16, is indeed 30. Then, provided by Annie's age 14, Anna's age is nine years plus half of 14. Half of 14 is 7, and when 9 is added to 7, we get 16.

We further conclude that Annie is 14 years old and Anna is 16 years old

19. Ano ang wu-wei ? ipalinawag ito


Answer:

Ang Wu Wei ay isang konsepto na literal na nangangahulugang "kawalang-kilos", "hindi pagkilos", o "walang kahirap-hirap na aksyon". Lumitaw kami noong panahon ng Spring at Autumn, at mula sa Confucianism, upang maging isang mahalagang konsepto sa statecraft ng Tsino at Taoism, at pinakakaraniwan ginamit upang tumukoy sa isang mainam na anyo ng pamahalaan, kabilang ang pag-uugali ng emperor. Na naglalarawan ng isang estado ng hindi nagagalit na personal na pagkakaisa, walang kusa na kusang-loob at savoir-faire, sa pangkalahatan ito ay mas maayos na nagsasaad ng isang estado ng espiritu o isipan, at sa Confucianism na naaayon sa maginoo na moralidad. Inilarawan ito ng Sinologist na si Jean François Billeter bilang isang "estado ng perpektong kaalaman sa katotohanan ng sitwasyon, perpektong pagiging mabisa at ang pagsasakatuparan ng isang perpektong ekonomiya ng enerhiya", na sa kasanayan Edward Slingerland kwalipikado bilang isang "hanay ng disposisyon  na umaayon sa normative order ".

Wu wei (Chinese: 無為; pinyin: wúwéi) is a concept literally meaning "inexertion", "inaction", or "effortless action".Wu wei emerged in the Spring and Autumn period, and from Confucianism, to become an important concept in Chinese statecraft and Taoism, and was most commonly used to refer to an ideal form of government,including the behavior of the emperor. Describing a state of unconflicting personal harmony, free-flowing spontaneity and savoir-faire, it generally also more properly denotes a state of spirit or mind, and in Confucianism accords with conventional morality. Sinologist Jean François Billeter describes it as a "state of perfect knowledge of the reality of the situation, perfect efficaciousness and the realization of a perfect economy of energy", which in practice Edward Slingerland qualifies as a "set of ('transformed') dispositions (including physical bearing)... conforming with the normative order".
