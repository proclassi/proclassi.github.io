10. 15. Will a family survive the future if domestic violence is on the rise? Why or why not?​


Answer:

Domestic violence can have a severe and lasting impact on families, and it can be difficult for a family to survive if it is on the rise. Domestic violence can cause physical, emotional, and psychological harm to family members, and can lead to an unstable and unhealthy home environment. Children who are exposed to domestic violence are more likely to experience behavioral and mental health problems, and are at a higher risk of becoming victims or perpetrators of domestic violence themselves in the future. The rise of domestic violence can also cause financial strain, as it can make it difficult for the primary breadwinner to work, and can lead to costly legal fees, medical bills, and housing expenses. Therefore, it is important for families to seek help and support if they are experiencing domestic violence, in order to address the issue and work towards creating a safer and healthier environment for all family members.

13. Why there was cases of domestic violence during coronavirus pandemic?​


There are increased cases of domestic violence during a pandemic because of stress, disruption of social and protective networks due to lockdown, increased economic hardship and decreased access to services.

In the case of COVID-19, isolation, restricted movement and stay-at-home measures or lockdown to contain the spread of the infection have a particularly acute impact on the victims.

In short, the chances of women and their children being exposed to violence is dramatically increased, as family members spend more time in close contact and household stress intensifies, and the risk grows even greater when families also have to cope with potential economic or job losses.

29. does marital abuse and domestic violence the same? ​


Answer:

When people think of domestic abuse, they often focus on domestic violence. But domestic abuse includes any attempt by one person in an intimate relationship or marriage to dominate and control the other. Domestic violence and abuse are used for one purpose and one purpose only: to gain and maintain total control over you. An abuser doesn’t “play fair.” An abuser uses fear, guilt, shame, and intimidation to wear you down and keep you under their thumb.

Domestic violence and abuse can happen to anyone; it does not discriminate. Abuse happens within heterosexual relationships and in same-sex partnerships. It occurs within all age ranges, ethnic backgrounds, and economic levels. And while women are more often victimized, men also experience abuse—especially verbal and emotional. The bottom line is that abusive behavior is never acceptable, whether from a man, woman, teenager, or an older adult. You deserve to feel valued, respected, and safe.

Explanation:

Domestic abuse often escalates from threats and verbal assault to violence. And while physical injury may pose the most obvious danger, the emotional and psychological consequences of domestic abuse are also severe. Emotionally abusive relationships can destroy your self-worth, lead to anxiety and depression, and make you feel helpless and alone. No one should have to endure this kind of pain—and your first step to breaking free is recognizing that your relationship is abusive.
