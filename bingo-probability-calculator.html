10. bingo uses the numbers 1-75, what is the probability it will not be a multiple of 5


Answer:

There are 15 multiples of 5 between 1 and 75: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35, 40, 45, 50, 55, 60, 65, 70, and 75.

The total number of possible numbers that could be called is 75. Of these, 15 are multiples of 5, leaving 60 that are not multiples of 5.

Therefore, the probability that a number called will not be a multiple of 5 is 60/75, or simplified, 4/5, or 0.8, or 80%.

19. in bingo game how many probability of picking a multiple 5


Answer:

There is a 20% probability of picking a multiple of 5 on any given draw.

Step-by-step explanation:

In a typical bingo game, there are 75 numbered balls. Of these 75 balls, there are 15 balls that are multiples of 5 (5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35, 40, 45, 50, 55, 60, 65, 70, 75).

So the probability of picking a multiple of 5 on a single draw is:

P(multiple of 5) = 15/75 = 1/5 = 0.2 = 20%
