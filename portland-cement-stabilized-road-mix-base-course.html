29. Why does a jeepney driver travel faster on a cemented road than on a bumpy road?​


Because on a concrete road, the tires always have friction. Therefore, they're always able to push off of the concrete and propel the car forward, which creates speed.

On a bumpy road, there isn't constant friction, so in the moments that the tires aren't touching or pushing away from anything, they're needlessly spinning and not accomplishing anything.
