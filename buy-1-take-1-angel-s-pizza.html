9. Chef Susan tosses some pizza dough in the air, then catches it again at the same height. The pizza dough is in the air for 1.0 s1.0\,\text s 1.0s 1, point, 0, start text, s, end text . We can ignore air resistance. What was the dough's velocity at the moment it was tossed into the air?


Answer:

The dough's velocity is 9.8 m/s.

Explanation:

Here, we are going to solve for the velocity of the dough the moment it was tossed into the air, meaning, we are referring to the initial velocity of the dough.

We will be applying the concept of Free falling bodies and the formula to be used is:

Vf = Vi + gt

where

Vf     is the final velocity, unit is in m/s

Vi     is the initial velocity, unit is in m/s

g      is the acceleration due to gravity, 9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

t       is the time covered by the motion of the object, unit is in seconds

For the given information

Vf = 0

t = 1.0 s

Here, we assume that at t = 1.0 s, the dough has reached its maximum height. At this point, the final velocity becomes zero (Vf = 0).

Solving the problem

Using the formula, let us solve for the initial velocity, we have:

Vf = Vi + gt

0 = Vi - (9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex])(1 s)

Take note that g is negative because the direction of the pull of gravity is downward.

Simplifying and transposing (9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex])(1 s) to the left side of the equation, we have:

Vi = 9.8 m/s

Therefore, the velocity of the dough is 9.8 m/s.

Answer:

Problem Involving Fractions

Tina had consumed the hawaiian flavored pizza.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given that 1/4 of the cheese flavored pizza was left, solve for the portion that were consumed. Since the denominator of 1/4 is 4 this means that the cheese pizza was divided into 4 equal parts which can be represented as 4/4. Subtract 1/4 from 4/4 to get the portion of the cheese pizza that were consumed thus, 4/4 - 1/4 = 3/4.

Given that 1/3 of the hawaiian flavored pizza was left, solve for the portion that were eaten. Since the denominator of 1/3 is 3 this means that the hawaiian pizza was divided into 3 equal parts which can be represented by 3/3. Subtract 1/3 from 3/3 to get the portion of the hawaiian pizza that were eaten thus, 3/3 - 1/3 = 2/3.

Given that 1/2 of the pepperoni flavored pizza was left, solve for the portion that was consumed. Since the denominator of 1/2 is 2 this means that the pepperoni pizza was divided into 2 equal parts which can be represented by 2/2. Subtract 1/2 from 2/2 to get the portion of the pepperoni pizza that was consumed thus 2/2 - 1/2 = 1/2.

Lastly, given that 5/8 of the vegetarian flavored pizza were left, solve for the portion that were consumed. since the denominator is 8 this means that the vegetarian pizza was divided into 8 equal parts which can be represented by 8/8. Subtract 5/8 from 8/8 to get the portion of the vegetarian pizza that were consumed thus 8/8 - 5/8 = 3/8.

Now, compare all the portions of each flavored pizza that were consumed: 3/4, 2/3, 1/2, and 3/8. Arrange these fractions from least to greatest: 3/8, 1/2, 2/3, 3/4. Given that Sara had eaten the least slices of pizza then it follows that it was the vegetarian pizza which she had consumed. 15. PROBLEMMOTHER had 4 slices of pizza. She gave 1/2 to het son and she ate 1/4 of it. What fraction of pizza left?


Answer:

3¼

Step-by-step explanation:

Add the taken parts of the pizza

[tex] = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{4} [/tex]

[tex] = \frac{1 \times 2}{2 \times 2} + \frac{1}{4} [/tex]

[tex] = \frac{2}{4} + \frac{1}{4} [/tex]

[tex] = \frac{3}{4} [/tex]

Subtract the sum from the whole.

[tex] = 4 - \frac{3}{4} [/tex]

[tex] = \frac{4}{1} - \frac{3}{4} [/tex]

[tex] = \frac{4 \times 4}{1 \times 4} - \frac{3}{4} [/tex]

[tex] = \frac{16}{4} - \frac{3}{4} [/tex]

[tex] = \frac{13}{4} [/tex]

[tex] = 3 \frac{1}{4} [/tex]
