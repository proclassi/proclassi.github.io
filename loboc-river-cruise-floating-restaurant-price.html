4. an 8-hour river cruise goes 12km upstream and then back. The speed of the river current is 2kph. What is the speed of the boat in still water?​


Answer:

4kph

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x be the speed of the boat

Speed of the river  = 2 kph

Total time spent = 8 hours

Distance = 12 km

Speed of the boat upstream ( against the river current) = x - 2

Speed of the boat downstream ( along with the current ) = x +2

The formula for t =  d/s

Where

d is distance

s is speed

Therefore the working equation would be

time for downstream + time for upstream = 8 hours

[tex]\frac{12}{x+2} + \frac{12}{x-2} = 8[/tex]

Multiplying both sides (x+2)(x-2) by  for the purpose of eliminating fractions

[tex]\frac{12(x+2)(x-2)}{x+2} + \frac{12(x+2)(x-2)}{x-2} = 8(x+2)(x-2)\\[/tex]

Cancel same terms

12(x-2) + 12(x+2) = 8(x+2)(x-2)

Solving further

[tex]12x-24+12x+24 = 8x^2 - 32[/tex]

[tex]24 x = 8x^2 - 32\\8x^2 -24x- 32 = 0\\x^2 - 3x -4 = 0\\\\\text {Factoring}\\(x-4)(x+1) = 0[/tex]

Finding the roots

x-4 = 0

x = 4

x+1 = 0

x = -1

Disregarding the negative value of x ,

The answer therefore is 4 kph for the speed of the boat in still water

Checking

[tex]\frac{12}{4+2} + \frac{12}{4-2} = 8\\2+6 =8\\8 =8[/tex]

6. 3-hour river cruise goes 15km upstream and then back again the river has a current of 2km an hour. What is the boat speed and how long was the upstream journey


Step-by-step explanation:

x = boat's speed

from the formula

d = st

d is distance

s is speed

t is time

t = d/s

upstream: (the boat is moving against the current)

t1 = 15/(x - 2)

downstream: (the boat is moving along with the current)

t2 = 15/(x + 2)

We know that the total travel time is 3 hours

t1 + t2 = 3

[15/(x - 2)] + [15/(x + 2)] = 3

15(x + 2) + 15(x - 2) = 3(x² - 4)

15x + 30 + 15x - 30 = 3x² - 12

30x = 3x² - 12

3x² - 30x - 12 = 0

x² - 10x - 4 = 0 (this quadratic equation cannot be factored, use quadratic formula)

x = 5 + √29 or 5 - √29

convert to decimal

x = 10.39 or -0.39

the most logical value for x is 10.39

x = 10.39

t2 = 15/(10.39 - 2) = 1.79

the boat's speed is: 10.39 km/hr

upstream travel time: 1.79 hours

20. Napakalinaw ng tubig sa Loboc River. Ano ang sagot? A. Lantay B. Pahambing C. Pasukdol​


Answer:

A lantay

Explanation:

walang paghahambing na pinapakita kaya lantay

Tanong:

Napakalinaw ng tubig sa Loboc River. Ano ang sagot?

A. Lantay B. Pahambing C. Pasukdol

Sagot:

C. Pasukdol

Repeated explanation:

Ang pasukdol ay ang paghahambing ng dalawa o higit pang bagay, tao, hayop at lugar. Ginagamit din ito sa mga salita na kadalasan nating nakikita. Lagi nating tatandaan na kapag nakakita tayo ng salitang (napaka) sa unahan ng salita na nito. Ibigsabihin, ito na ay ang pasukdol. Meron ring mga uri ang pang uri. Ito ay ang tinatawag na pasukdol, pahambing at lantay

Lantay:

Ito ay hindi na ginagamitan ng salitang (Napaka, Mas, at iba pa). Para rin itong normal na salita na ginagamit kadalasan sa mga pangungusap.

Pahambing:

Ito po ay isang uri ng pang uri na kung saan naghahambing ito ng dalawa lamang. Ginagamitan rin ito ng salitang (mas).

Pasukdol:

Ito ay kayang maghambing ng dalawa o higit pa. Kadalsang ginagamitan ito ng salitang (Pinaka).

29. ano ang loboc river ​


Answer: Ang Ilog Loboc o ang Loboc River ay isang ilog sa lalawigan ng Bohol,

Answer:

The Loboc River (also called Loay River[1]) is a river in the Bohol province of the Philippines. It is one of the major tourist destinations of Bohol.

Explanation:

The source of the Loboc River is located in the town of Loboc, almost in the center of Bohol. From Loboc, the river takes a westerly course for a distance of about 1.5 kilometres (1 mi) then flows due south into the Mindanao or Bohol Sea. Its drainage area of approximately, 520 square kilometres (200 sq mi), is bordered by a horseshoe-shaped chain of mountain peaks rising to an elevation of 800 metres (2,600 ft). Rainfall is distributed almost uniformly throughout the year, thus Loboc River has a steady and high base flow.
