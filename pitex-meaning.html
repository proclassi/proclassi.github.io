5. jona has a size of a piexe of carolina paper 60cm by 20cm what is the remaining uncut paper if the circles are 10cm​


Answer:

289.88 cm².

Step-by-step explanation:

To calculate the area of the paper, we need to use the formula for the area of a rectangle:

Area of the paper = length x width

Area of the paper = 60 cm x 20 cm

Area of the paper = 1200 cm²

To calculate the area of one circle, we use the formula for the area of a circle:

Area of a circle = π x (radius)²

The radius of the circle is 10 cm, so:

Area of one circle = π x (10 cm)²

Area of one circle = π x 100 cm²

Area of one circle = 100π cm²

To calculate the number of circles that can be cut from the paper, we need to divide the area of the paper by the area of one circle:

Number of circles = Area of the paper / Area of one circle

Number of circles = 1200 cm² / (100π cm²)

Number of circles ≈ 3.8197

Since we can only cut a whole number of circles, Jona can cut only 3 circles from the paper. The area of three circles is:

Area of three circles = 3 x (Area of one circle)

Area of three circles = 3 x (100π cm²)

Area of three circles = 300π cm²

The remaining uncut paper is the difference between the area of the paper and the area of the circles:

Remaining uncut paper = Area of the paper - Area of three circles

Remaining uncut paper = 1200 cm² - 300π cm²

Remaining uncut paper ≈ 289.88 cm² (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, the remaining uncut paper is approximately 289.88 cm².
