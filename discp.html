15. In a discus throw competition, an athlete throws a 2 kg metal disc with an initial velocity of 30 m/s at 40 degrees from the horizontal. What are the kinetic and potential energy of the metal disc (a) 1 s, (b) 2 s, and (c) * 3 after the throw? (d) Is the total mechanical energy conserved at these times? Justify your answer.​


The athlete threw the disc with an initial velocity of 30 meters per second at a height of 40 meters. The kinetic energy was calculated using KE = ½mv2. At t=1 second, the kinetic energy of the disc was 0.5 x (30)(20)=60 J. At t=2 seconds, the kinetic energy of the disc was 60 +0.5x(30)(22)=90J. At t*3, the kinetic energy of the disc was 90+0.5x(30)(24)=120 J. Thus, the total kinetic energy of the disc was 120 J. This means that there was no change in the total kinetic energy of the disc. Therefore, the total mechanical energy was constant during this period.
