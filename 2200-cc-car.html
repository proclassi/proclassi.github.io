Problem:

A 1800 kg car is moving at 30 m/s eastward. It collides head on with a 2200 kg garbage truck. what is the initial velocity of the truck if the car and the truck move together after collision at 12 m/s?

Note:

Total momentum of the system is conserved before and after collision. Therefore,

Total momentum (before collision) = Total momentum after collision

Given Data

                                   mass                       velocity (before collision)

car                           1800 kg                   30 m/s eastward

garbage truck        2200 kg                      v1 =    ? unknown

Solution:

Total momentum (before collision) = Total momentum after collision

(1800 kg × 30 m/s) + (2200 kg × v1) = (1800 kg × 12 m/s) + (2200 kg × 12 m/s)

     54000 kg.m/s  + (2200 kg × v1) = 21600 kg.m/s + 26400 kg.m/s

                                  (2200 kg × v1) = 48000 kg.m/s - 54000 kg.m/s

                                                    v1  = - 6000 kg.m/s / 2200 kg

                                                    v1 =  - 2.727 m/s

The velocity of the garbage truck before collision is = - 2.727 m/s since its direction is opposite to the direction of the car.

12. A 1800 kg car is moving at 30 m/s eastward. It collides head on with a 2200 kg garbage truck. what is the initial velocity of the truck if the car and the truck move together after collision at 12 m/s? (show your complete solution) *


Answer: 2.727 m/s west

Solution:

Given:  mass of car (M1)=1800 kg

            velocity of car (V1) =30 m/s

            mass of truck (M2)=2200 kg

            the car and the truck move together after collision at 12 m/s (Vf)

Required: velocity of truck (V2)= ?

Description: This is a momentum question

Use formula: (M1) (V1) + (M2) (V2) = (mass total) (Vf)

(1800 kg) (30 m/s) + (2200 kg) (V2) = (1800 + 2200) (12 m/s)

V2 = -2.727 m/s (negative because the direction of the truck is opposite of the car)

V2 = 2.727 m/s west

14. A 1800-kg car is moving at 30 m/s eastward. It collides head on with a 2200- kg garbage truck. What is the initial velocity of the truck if the car and the truck move together after collision at 12 m/s?


We use the equation for ineffective collision here, since the car and the truck are moving together.

[tex]{m}_{car}{{v}_{car}}_{i} \: + \: {m}_{truck}{{v}_{truck}}_{i} \: = ({m}_{car} + {m}_{truck}){v}_{f}[/tex]

We substitute their values in the equation, leaving our truck's initial velocity as our unknown value, [tex]{{v}_{truck}}_{i}[/tex].

[tex](1800 \: kg)(30 \: m/s) + (2200 \: kg)({{v}_{truck}}_{i}) = (1800 + 2200 \: kg)(12 \: m/s)[/tex]

Solve.

[tex]{{v}_{truck}}_{i}= \frac{(1800 + 2200 \: kg)(12 \: m/s) - (1800 \: kg)(30 \: m/s)}{2200 \: kg} [/tex]

[tex]{{v}_{truck}}_{i} = - 2.72 \: m/s[/tex]

The negative sign on the truck's initial velocity simply indicates that it is moving to the opposite direction of the car.

Applying proper significant figures, our truck's initial velocity would be [tex] 2.7 \: m/s[/tex] westward.
