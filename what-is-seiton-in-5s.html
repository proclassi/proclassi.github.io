19. What is Seiton? What are the things you do and setting the right order?​


Answer:

Seiton isa Japanese term that refers to the practice of organizing and arranging items ina specific order to improve efficiency and productivity. It involves identifying the necessary items, removing any unnecessary items, and arranging the remaining items in a logical and accessible manner. The goal is to create a clean and organized workspace that promotes efficiency and reduces waste.

Seiton is a Japanese term that means "sorting" or "organizing" in the context of workplace organization and efficiency. It is one of the 5S principles of Lean Manufacturing, which is a methodology for optimizing workplace organization and efficiency.

In Seiton, the goal is to arrange items in the workplace in the most efficient way possible, so that they are easy to access, use, and put away. This involves setting things in the right order, so that everything has a designated place and is easy to find when needed. Some of the things you can do to apply Seiton in the workplace are:

1. Determine what items are necessary and discard what is not needed.
2. Identify a specific location for each item.
3. Arrange items in a logical sequence that makes sense for the workflow.
4. Use labels, signs, or color-coding to identify and distinguish items from each other.
5. Ensure that everything has a proper place and is returned to its designated location after use.
6. Review and update the organization system periodically to ensure it remains effective.

Overall, Seiton helps create a clean and well-organized workplace, which promotes productivity, efficiency, and safety.

23. what does seiton means​


Answer:

Seiton (systematise) means proper arrangement. Seiso (clean) implies keeping things clean and polished in the workplace. Seiketsu implies purity and focuses on maintaining cleanliness and perpetual cleaning.


24. what is Seiton means?​


Answer:

Seiton means determining where and when items are needed, and placing them in a way that promotes efficient work flow. A worker should not have to hunt for necessary tools, or walk to another area to access often-used items.
