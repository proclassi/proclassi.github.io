Since the denominator of 1/4 is 4 this means that the cheese pizza was divided into 4 equal parts which can be represented as 4/4. Subtract 1/4 from 4/4 to get the portion of the cheese pizza that were consumed thus, 4/4 - 1/4 = 3/4.

Given that 1/3 of the hawaiian flavored pizza was left, solve for the portion that were eaten. Since the denominator of 1/3 is 3 this means that the hawaiian pizza was divided into 3 equal parts which can be represented by 3/3. Subtract 1/3 from 3/3 to get the portion of the hawaiian pizza that were eaten thus, 3/3 - 1/3 = 2/3.

Given that 1/2 of the pepperoni flavored pizza was left, solve for the portion that was consumed. Since the denominator of 1/2 is 2 this means that the pepperoni pizza was divided into 2 equal parts which can be represented by 2/2. 9. You are trying to decide which pizza to order for dinner. Your choices for crust are regular, thin, or deep dish. Your options for meat are pepperoni or sausage. Your options for the veggie are onion or green pepper. If you want two toppings, one meat and one veggie, how many possible combinations of pizza can you order?


Answer:

12

Step-by-step explanation:

In order to determine the possible combinations of problems like these, we simply multiply the number of choices per category.

Total Combinations = # Crust * #Meat * #Veggie

Note the cardinality, or number of elements per set

Crust = {Regular, Thin, Deep Dish}               |Crust| = 3

Meat = {Pepperoni, Sausage}                       |Meat| = 2

Veggie = {Onion, Green Pepper}                 |Veggie| = 2

Substitute,

Total Combinations = 3 * 2 * 2

Total Combinations = 12

There are 12 different pizzas you can order.

26. You decided to order a pizza but you have to choose the type of crust and the toppings. If there are only 6 possible combinations of ordering a pizza, from which of the following should you choose?a. Crust: thin or deep dish Topping: cheese or pepperonib. Crust: thin or deep dish Topping: cheese, bacon, or pepperonic. Crust: thin or deep dish Topping: cheese, bacon, sausage, or pepperonid. Crust: thin or deep dish Topping: cheese, bacon, sausage, pepperoni, or hotdog


Answer:

B

Step-by-step explanation:

The number of possible combinations of ordering a pizza given a number of choices of type of crust and a number of choices of toppings could be solved by the Fundamental Counting Principle, in which we multiply separate events together to know the number of all possible outcomes.

"If there are only 6 possible combinations of ordering a pizza" means that the product of the number of types of crust multiplied by the number of choices of toppings must be equal to 6.

Going over each of the choices:

a. 2 types crust x 2 choices of toppings = 4 possible combinations

b. 2 types crust x 3 choices of toppings = 6 possible combinations

c. 2 types crust x 4 choices of toppings = 8 possible combinations
