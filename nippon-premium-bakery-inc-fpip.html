what are the default risk premium?

A default premium is an additional amount that a borrower must pay to compensate a lender for assuming default risk. All companies or borrowers indirectly pay a default premium, though the rate at which they must repay the obligation varies.

explanation;

Answer:

576kg of flour

Step-by-step explanation:

Bakery B:

x kg

Bakery A:

(x-12) kg

Bakery C:

2(x-12+x) kg = 2(2x-12) kg

Solve for x:

936=x+x-12+2(2x-12)

936=2x-12+4x-24

936=6x-36

936=6(x-6)

156=x-6

150=x

Substitute the value of x to each term:

Bakery B:

x kg = 150kg

Bakery A:

(x-12) kg = 138kg

Bakery C:

2(2x-12) kg = 576kg

Bakery C used 576kg of flour.

29. The premium on an insurance policy is 20% for the first year, 15% on the second year, and 10% for the third year. Find the total premium paid for a policy of $40,000.


Answer:

The first year premium would be 20% of $40,000, or $8,000.

The second year premium would be 15% of $40,000, or $6,000.

The third year premium would be 10% of $40,000, or $4,000.

The total premium paid over the three years would be $8,000 + $6,000 + $4,000 = $18,000.

Step-by-step explanation:

1. First, calculate the premium for the first year by multiplying the policy amount by the percentage: $40,000 x 20% = $8,000

2. Next, calculate the premium for the second year by multiplying the policy amount by the percentage: $40,000 x 15% = $6,000

3. Then, calculate the premium for the third year by multiplying the policy amount by the percentage: $40,000 x 10% = $4,000

4. Finally, add up the premiums from each year to find the total premium paid: $8,000 + $6,000 + $4,000 = $18,000
