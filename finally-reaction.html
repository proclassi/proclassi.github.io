13. 15.0 grams of C2H6 was mixed with 40.0 grams of O2in a 50.0 L tank and ignited with an electric spark. Assuming that there are no other reaction products, and that the reaction goes to completion (i.e. all the limiting reactant is used up), calculate the final pressure, in atmospheres, in the tank at a final temperature of 150 °C.


Answer:0.496 atm

Explanation:

To calculate for the final pressure, we are going to use the ideal gas equation. But first, we have to find the number of moles of carbon dioxide produced. And to find the number of moles of carbon dioxide, we must first find the limiting reactant.

To start, write first the balanced chemical equation,

C2H6 + 3.5O2---> 2CO2 + 3H2O

Now, the next step is to find the limiting reactant. A detailed process how to determine the limiting reactant can be found here: https://brainly.ph/question/81152

For this problem, the limiting reactant is oxygen. Therefore,

[tex]40 g O_{2} (\frac{1 mol O_{2} }{32 g}) (\frac{2 mol CO_{2} }{3.5 mol O_{2} } )\\=0.714 mol CO2[/tex]

Using the obtained value for the number of moles of CO2, we can then use the ideal gas equation to find the final pressure of the gas inside the tank,

PV=nRT

P=nRT/V

={(0.714 mol)(0.0821 L-atm/mol-K)(150 + 273.15K)}/50 L

P=0.496 atm

Therefore, the final pressure of the gas inside the tank is 0.496 atm.
