Speed Word Problems

1. Asked: What was Marvin's average rate of speed?

Given: 5 hours and 120 km

Solution: 120 km ÷ 5 hrs = 24 km/h

Final Answer: Marvin's average rate of speed is 24 km/h.

2. Asked: What was the average speed of the taxi?

Given: 8:00 am, 8:30 am and 13 km

Solution: 8:30 am - 8:00 am = 30 mins

13 km ÷ 30 mins = 0.43 km/min

Final Answer: The average speed of the taxi is 0.43 km/min.

Step-by-Step Explanation:

How To Find The Speed

The word problems above are asking for the speed. The speed refers to how fast an object or a person moves from one place to another. To find the speed, you have to divide the distance by the time.

speed = distance ÷ time

The distance refers to how much ground has been covered during the motion and the time refers to how long the motion takes. Below are the formulas for finding the distance and time:

distance = speed × time

time = distance ÷ speed

15. 1:Marvin took a 5 hour bicycle trip in all he traveled 120 km what was his average rate of speed?Asked:Given:Solution:2:a taxi cab left tabunok at 8:00 a.m. and arrived at cebu city at 8:30 a.m. if the distance between the two places is 13 kilometers m what was the averadge speed of the taxi?Asked:Given:Solution:​


Word Problems on Speed

1. Asked: Marvin's average rate of speed.

  Given: 5 hours - time it took Marvin complete a bicycle trip

           120 km - distance Marvin had covered biking

  Solution: 120 km ÷ 5 hrs = 24 km/hr

Therefore, Marvin's average rate of speed is 24 km/hr.

2. Asked: The average speed of the taxi.

   Given: 8:00 am - the time the taxi cab left Tabunok

               8:30 am - the time the taxi cab has arrived at Cebu City

               13 kms - the distance between Tabunok and Cebu City

   Solution: 8: 30 am - 8: 00 am = 30 minutes

                    30 minutes = [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex]hr

                    13 kms ÷ [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex]hr = [tex]\frac{(13)(2)}{1}[/tex] km/hr = 26 km/hr

Therefore, the average speed of the taxi is 26 km/hr.
