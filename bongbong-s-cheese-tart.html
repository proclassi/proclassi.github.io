20. Cedrick packed 15 tarts in each of his 30 plastic boxes and had 15 tarts left on a tray. He decided to repack all the tarts into paper boxes such that each box contained 12 tarts, and put the remaining tarts aside. a. If he sold the box for Php129.00, how much did he get for selling all the boxes he had packed? b. How many more tarts must he bake in addition to the remaining tarts, to collect another Php258.00?​


Answer:

a. Php59,985.00b. Php258.00

Step-by-step explanation:

a. To determine how many boxes Cedrick had packed, we can use division:

Number of boxes = Total tarts packed / Tarts per box

Number of boxes = (15 tarts/box x 30 boxes) + 15 tarts

Number of boxes = 465 boxes

To determine the total amount of money Cedrick earned from selling the boxes, we can multiply the number of boxes by the selling price per box:

Total earnings = Number of boxes x Selling price per box

Total earnings = 465 boxes x Php129.00/box

Total earnings = Php59,985.00

Therefore, Cedrick earned Php59,985.00 from selling all the boxes he had packed.

b. Cedrick has 15 + 465 x 12 = 5,595 tarts in total (15 leftover tarts plus the 465 boxes that contained 12 tarts each).

To determine how many more tarts Cedrick needs to bake in order to collect Php258.00, we can use the following equation:

Additional tarts = Additional amount / Price per tart

Additional tarts = Php258.00 / Php129.00 per box x 12 tarts per box

Additional tarts = 24 tarts

Therefore, Cedrick needs to bake 24 more tarts in addition to the remaining tarts he has to collect another Php258.00.
