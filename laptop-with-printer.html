29. D. 12 5. In every 2 printers there are 3 laptops. If there are 6 printers, how many laptops are there?​


RATIO AND PROPORTION APPLICATION

Problem:

In every 2 printers, there are 3 laptops. If there are 6 printers, how many laptops are there?​

Answer: There would be 9 laptops when there were 6 printers.

Step-by-step explanation:

In order to answer this question involving the application of ratio and proportion, you should know the difference of the two terms. Ratio refers to the relation of two different quantities while proportion refers to the ratio of two ratios. In the problem, the ratio is 2 printers : 3 laptops. This is read as "Two printers is to three laptops". On the other hand, the proportion is 2 printers : 3 laptops :: 6 printers : x laptops. This is read as "Two printers is to three laptops as 6 printers is to x laptops" where x refers to the unknown number of laptops.

Remember that in a proportion, the means are the middle or inner quantities while the extremes are the outer quantities, and the rule is that the product of the means is equal to the product of the extremes. In the given:

2 : 3 :: 6 : x

3 and 6 are the means while 2 and x are the extremes. To solve for the value of x, simply multiply 3 and 6 then divide 2.

3 times 6 is 18. 18 divided by 2 is 9.

Hence, the answer is 9 laptops.
