Answer:

Geometry: A branch of mathematics concerned with the study of space and the relationships between points, lines, angles, and shapes.

Point: A location in space with no size or dimension, represented by a dot in a geometric figure.

Line: A straight path that extends indefinitely in both directions and has no width or thickness.

Segment: A part of a line with two endpoints.

Ray: A part of a line that starts at one endpoint and extends indefinitely in one direction.

Plane: A flat, two-dimensional surface that extends infinitely in all directions.

Intersecting Lines: Two lines that cross each other at a single point.

Parallel Lines: Two lines that never intersect and are always the same distance apart.

Skew Lines: Two lines that are not parallel and do not intersect.

Perpendicular Lines: Two lines that intersect at a 90-degree angle.

Coplanar Points: Points that lie on the same plane.

Collinear Points: Points that lie on the same line.

Coplanar Lines: Lines that lie on the same plane.

Angles: The amount of rotation between two rays with a common endpoint, measured in degrees.

Complementary Angles: Two angles whose sum is 90 degrees.

Supplementary Angles: Two angles whose sum is 180 degrees.

Adjacent Angles: Two angles that share a common vertex and a common side but do not overlap.

Vertical Angles: Two angles that are opposite each other and formed by intersecting lines.

Right Angles: Angles that measure exactly 90 degrees.

Obtuse Angle: An angle that measures greater than 90 degrees but less than 180 degrees.

Acute Angles: Angles that measure less than 90 degrees.
