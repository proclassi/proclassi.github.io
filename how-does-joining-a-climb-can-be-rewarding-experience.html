[tex]\boxed{P=\dfrac{W}{t}=\frac{PE}{t}=\frac{mgh}{t} }[/tex]

where

P = power

m = mass of the object

g = acceleration due to gravity

h = height

The boy who takes to climb the rope has the time of

[tex]t=\dfrac{mgh}{P}\\\\t=\dfrac{(45.0\text{ kg})(9.8\text{ m/s}^2)(7.0\text{ m})}{350\text{ W}} \\\\\boxed{t=8.82\text{ s}}[/tex]

27. A 45.0 kg boy climbs 7.0m up rope. If theboy's power is 350 W, how long does it takes aboy to climb the rope?​


Answer:

In climbing the rope, the boy gains potential energy Ep equal to:

Ep=mgh=(45)(9.81)(7.0)=3090 joules

Given his power P , the time t it takes him to climb the rope is:

t=EpP=3090350

= 8.83 seconds

This assumes that the boy started and ended the climb with the same kinetic energy. If he accelerated during the climb, the stated power is assumed to be an average.
