1. 3 examples of evidence that are not competent and excluded by law or the rules.​


Answer:

Hearsay evidence, which is a statement made by a person who is not present in court and is not under oath, is generally not considered competent evidence and is typically excluded by law.

Evidence obtained through illegal means, such as through an illegal search or seizure, is typically not considered competent and is excluded by law.

Evidence that is irrelevant to the case at hand is typically not considered competent and is excluded by the rules of evidence.


2. Cite at least 3 examples of evidence that are not competent and excluded by law or the rules.​


Answer:

1. Hearsay evidence: Hearsay evidence is testimony given by a witness who did not directly observe the events but instead heard about them from someone else. It is generally not admissible in court because it is considered unreliable.

2. Opinion evidence: Opinion evidence is testimony given by a witness regarding their personal beliefs, assumptions, or interpretations of the facts. It is not admissible unless the witness has expertise in the subject matter.

3. Character evidence: Character evidence is testimony or evidence presented to show that a person acted in a certain way based on their general reputation or past behavior. It is not admissible to prove that a person acted in a certain way in a specific instance.

10. what cooking methods are suitable for tender cuts of meat or less tender cuts that have been mmarinate?


Answer:

Dry Heat- Dry heat methods of cooking are suitable for tender cuts of meat or less tender cuts which have been marinated. Dry heat methods include roasting, oven broiling, grilling, pan-broiling, panfrying and stir—frying.
