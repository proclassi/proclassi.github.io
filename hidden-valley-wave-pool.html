13. A light wave traveling in air passes into the water in a swimming pool at an angle of incidence of 35˚. Calculate the angle of refraction of the light in water.​


Answer:

According to Snell's law of refraction, the ratio of the sines of the angles of incidence and refraction is equal to the ratio of the velocities of light in the two media:

sin(incidence angle) / sin(refraction angle) = velocity of light in air / velocity of light in water

Assuming the velocity of light in air is approximately equal to its value in a vacuum, which is 3 x 10^8 m/s, and the velocity of light in water is 2.25 x 10^8 m/s, we can plug in the values and solve for the angle of refraction:

sin(35˚) / sin(refraction angle) = (3 x 10^8 m/s) / (2.25 x 10^8 m/s)

sin(refraction angle) = sin(35˚) x (2.25 x 10^8 m/s) / (3 x 10^8 m/s)

sin(refraction angle) = 0.47

refraction angle = sin^-1(0.47)

refraction angle ≈ 28.4˚

Therefore, the angle of refraction of the light in water is approximately 28.4˚.


14. Two water waves in a pool have amplitudes of 0.5 m and 0.25 m, respectively. The energy carried by the wave whose amplitude is 0.5 m is found to be 4.0 J. How much energy is carried by the other wave?


The energy carried by a wave is proportional to the square of its amplitude. Therefore, the energy carried by the wave with an amplitude of 0.25 m can be calculated using the following equation:

(Energy 1 / Energy 2) = (Amplitude 1^2 / Amplitude 2^2)

Solving for Energy 2:

Energy 2 = Energy 1 * (Amplitude 2^2 / Amplitude 1^2)

Energy 2 = 4.0 J * (0.25^2 / 0.5^2)

Energy 2 = 1.0 J

Therefore, the energy carried by the other wave with an amplitude of 0.25 m is 1.0 J.

Answer:

B. longitude and latitudes

Explanation:

bcoz napagaralan na namin ito when I was grade 7

There are two types of waves: Longitudinal waves and Transverse waves.

Longitudinal Waves: A wave in which the particles of the medium vibrate back and forth in the ‘same direction’ in which the wave is moving. Medium can be solid, liquid or gases. Therefore, sound waves are longitudinal waves.

Transverse Waves: A wave in which the particles of the medium vibrate up and down ‘at right angles’ to the direction in which the wave is moving. These waves are produced only in a solids and liquids but not in gases.

Sound is a longitudinal wave which consists of compressions and rarefactions travelling through a medium.

Answer:

GIVEN: d (1-way) =25 m, t (2-way)=25 s

Find v.

If the pool is 25 meters long, then the back-and-forth distance is 50 meters. The wave covers this distance in 25 seconds. Now use v = d / t.

v = d / t = (50 m) / (25 s) = 2 m/s
