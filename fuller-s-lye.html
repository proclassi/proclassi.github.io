22. what is the difference between fuller technique and manungko approach?​


The Fuller Technique and Manungko Approach are both methods used in poultry farming to raise native chicken breeds. The main difference between the two is in their approach to feeding and managing the birds.

The Fuller Technique, developed by American poultry scientist Lewis Ful l er, involves feeding the birds with a diet that consists of locally available ingredients such as rice bran, copra meal, and fish meal. The birds are raised in confinement and provided with a balanced diet that meets their nutritional needs. This technique is designed to maximize growth and weight gain in the birds, with the goal of producing meat for commercial purposes.

On the other hand, the Manungko Approach, developed by the Philippine National Agriculture and Fishery Council, emphasizes the use of free-range and organic farming methods. The chickens are allowed to roam freely and forage for their own food, which includes grasses, insects, and other small animals. This approach promotes a natural and sustainable way of raising chickens, with a focus on producing high-quality meat and eggs.

In summary, the Fuller Technique focuses on maximizing growth and weight gain through a controlled diet in a confined space, while the Manungko Approach prioritizes free-range and organic farming methods that promote natural and sustainable chicken raising practices.

5 steps to a Fuller Life in your own words

1.Decide what's most important to you. Now that you've passed middle age, you really don't need to be wasting time on things you don't enjoy. That means you need to figure out what you do enjoy spending your time on. Maybe you love throwing yourself into your work, or maybe your home life is more where you're focused. Maybe you love to travel and have adventures. Whatever it is, work on figuring out what you enjoy the most.

Spend some time writing about when you feel the happiest. Ask yourself, "What brings me the most joy?" Another good question to ask yourself is, "If tomorrow were my last day on earth, what would I want to do?"

2.Set boundaries. Having healthy boundaries can help you to feel happier, especially if your boundaries are not as firm as you’d like them to be. Work on setting good boundaries in your personal and professional relationships, such as by telling people what your boundaries are and reminding them if they cross your boundaries.

3.Be assertive. Being assertive in the way that you communicate with other people can also help you to feel happier past middle age. Work on developing your assertive communication skills as well, such as by expressing what you want in a direct manner and saying “No” when you cannot or do not want to do something.

4.Put your priorities first. Once you realize what's important to you, you need to work to rearrange your life to put that first. If family is your first priority, then consider limiting your work schedule if possible. If you love to travel, consider limiting other expenses so you can travel more often

5.Learn to stand up for what you believe in. Now that you're past middle age, you've probably sorted out your values. However, you may not have always stood up for those values. Now is the time to start doing it. You have age and experience on your side.[3]

For instance, if you feel something is unethical at work, say so. Of course, you want to be civil, but you have the experience to speak up for yourself.When you see injustice, fight against it.
