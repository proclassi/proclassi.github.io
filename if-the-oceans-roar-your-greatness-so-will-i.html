22. What is meant by the line "The Roaring Twenties" was put to a halt because of the Great Depression?


Answer:

"The Roaring Twenties was put to a halt because of the Great Depression" means that the decade of economic growth in 1920s was stopped because of economic crisis.

Explanation:

A. Roaring Twenties

Pertains to the economic growth and prosperity in the decade of 1920 In America and Europe and other developed countries Automobiles, electricity and other businesses experienced growth and expansion Stock prices continuously increase Many Americans were employed Goods such as appliances and furniture flowed out of factories

B. Halt

Means bring or come to stop

C. Great Depression

Began in 1929 Started on October 29, 1929 when New York Stock Exchange crashed Mass production of goods Unequal distribution of wealth Stock market began to spiral downward Worldwide economic downturn Laissez-faire approach by Presidents: Warren G. Harding (1921–23), Calvin Coolidge (1923–29), and Herbert Hoover (1929–33) Laissez-faire mans the deliberate absence of government regulation They did not regulate: the buying or selling of stocks and bonds; banking, manufacturing, or agricultural production Unsound banking system This period would last until 1941

Roaring Twenties refers to the decade during 1920 in the society and culture in the Western. It was pertaining to the culture and and economic prosperity on the countries in Europe and United States. Major cities such as Berlin, Chicago, London, New York City, Paris and Sydney experienced the Roaring Twenties. It began in leading metropolitan centers and spread and widely in the aftermath of World War 1. In the world of finance, United States gained its dominance.
