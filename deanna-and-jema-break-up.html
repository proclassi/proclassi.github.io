Answer:

To find the area of the poster, we need to first find the area of the rectangle and then subtract the areas of the two right triangles.

The area of the rectangle is:

a x b = 10 cm x 26 cm = 260 cm^2

To find the area of the right triangles, we need to first find their heights using the Pythagorean theorem. For the right triangle on the left, we have:

h^2 = c^2 - a^2

h^2 = 15^2 - 10^2

h^2 = 225 - 100

h^2 = 125

h = sqrt(125)

h = 11.18 cm

The area of the right triangle on the left is:

(1/2) x a x h = (1/2) x 10 cm x 11.18 cm = 55.9 cm^2

For the right triangle on the right, we have:

h^2 = d^2 - b^2

h^2 = 20^2 - 26^2

h^2 = 400 - 676

h^2 = 276

h = sqrt(276)

h = 16.64 cm

The area of the right triangle on the right is:

(1/2) x b x h = (1/2) x 26 cm x 16.64 cm = 217.28 cm^2

Therefore, the area of the poster is:

260 cm^2 - 55.9 cm^2 - 217.28 cm^2 = 430.82 cm^2

Rounding to the nearest whole number, we get 431 cm^2.

Therefore, the answer is A. 430 cm^2.
