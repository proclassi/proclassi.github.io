14. What is the significance of Zechariah's loss of speech before the birth of John?


Answer:

Zechariah's canticle serves as an affirmation of his faith experience since he lost his speech due to his unbelief at the Angel Gabriel's announcement that his wife, who ways thought to be barren, is found to be miraculously with a child. His loss of speech is symbolic of Zechariah's reluctance to believe in God's mysterious ways which was to him, restored during the birth of John.


15. In the canticle of Zechariah, John the Baptist is described as what?


Answer:

The Benedictus (also Song of Zechariah or Canticle of Zachary), given in Gospel of Luke 1:68–79, is one of the three canticles in the first two chapters of this Gospel, the other two being the "Magnificat" and the "Nunc dimittis". The Benedictus was the song of thanksgiving uttered by Zechariah on the occasion of the circumcision of his son, John the Baptist.
