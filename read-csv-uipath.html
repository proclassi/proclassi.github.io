5. Which of the following database concepts would most likely use a csv file?


A CSV file, or comma-separated values file, enables the saving of data in a tabular format. With the addition of the csv extension, CSVs resemble standard spreadsheets. Most spreadsheet programs, including Microsoft Excel and Google Spreadsheets, support the use of CSV files. Due to the fact that only one sheet may be saved at a time and no cells, columns, or rows can be saved, they are different from other spreadsheet file types. Additionally, formulas in this format cannot be saved.

There are numerous business uses for these files. For instance, they assist businesses in exporting a huge amount of data to a database with fewer records. They also perform the following two essential business functions:

CSV files can be created more easily by a website developer because they are plain-text filesWhatever the software you're using, since they're plain text, they're simpler to import into a spreadsheet or another storage database.To better organize massive amounts of data
