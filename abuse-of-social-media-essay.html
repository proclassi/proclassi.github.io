8. how is honesty violated and abused in media and communication?​


The Supreme Court said in the Tinker case that “…conduct by the student, in class or out of it, which for any reason - whether it stems from time, place, or type of behavior - materially disrupts class work or involves substantial disorder or invasion of the rights of others is, of course, not immunized by the constitutional guarantee of freedom of speech.” This is the standard to be applied when an educational institution is alleging that a student engaged in disruptive speech that the institution would like to halt.

In the later case of Bethel School District v. Fraser, the Court ruled that a school was not violating a student's rights when it suspended a student for the use of crude language in a speech to a school assembly. The Supreme Court said, “It does not follow … that simply because the use of an offensive form of expression may not be prohibited to adults making what the speaker considers a political point, the same latitude must be permitted to children in a public school… The determination of what manner of speech in the classroom or in school assembly is inappropriate properly rests with the school board.”

Social media is very easy to access nowadays. It has given us people the freedom to express our views and opinions freely and sometimes anonymously. Honestly, I think that it has turned to one of those things that we consider a “double-edged sword” as it both has it’s positive and negative sides.


The President’s current campaign against drugs is a very controversial matter. There is never a day that I don’t see an article or a piece of news about it as I scroll mindlessly to numerous ads and posts by my “online” friends. Personally, I think it’s great as we get updated and we can view videos and pictures easily. It gives transparency in some sort of way as CCTV footages are released in facebook, instagram and twitter in a matter of seconds (especially now that drug abuse is often linked to killings). The only downside that I can think of is the rampant posting of fake news. If there are pages that are legitimate, I am a hundred percent sure that there are also people out there who will go on great lengths to create a page that will mislead the public too.


Overall, I think that it is also our responsibility as citizens or netizens to learn how to decipher the truth from the lies. If we will only be vigilant in doing so, then social media’s good effects have definitely outweighed the bad.
